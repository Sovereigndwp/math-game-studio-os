\## Game Concept: **Echo Heist**

\**Genre:** Co-op (or solo) stealth-strategy roguelite  
\**Hook:** You can **record a 10–20 second “echo” of your actions**, then **replay it as an AI ghost teammate** to run synchronized heists.

\### Core Fantasy
You’re a crew of time-bending thieves breaking into procedural facilities. Instead of hiring NPCs, you **create your own crew** by recording echoes: one echo distracts guards, another hacks doors, another carries loot—while you play the “current” thief.

\---

\## Gameplay Loop (10–15 minutes per run)
1\. **Scout Phase (real-time stealth)**  
   Learn guard routes, camera cones, door locks, noise surfaces.
2\. **Plan Phase (quick tactical setup)**  
   Place simple waypoints + choose roles for your next echo (Decoy/Hacker/Runner).
3\. **Execute Phase (echo orchestration)**  
   \- Record an echo (10–20s): you sneak in, swipe a keycard, open a vent.  
   \- Rewind to start of execute: your echo repeats that path perfectly.  
   \- You act simultaneously to chain actions (double keycard doors, timed switches, multi-room disables).
4\. **Escape + Score**  
   Cash out loot, earn upgrades, unlock new gadgets and facility “biomes”.

\---

\## Key Mechanics
\### 1) Echo Recording
\- One button starts recording; another ends it.
\- Echo repeats exactly, but **reacts minimally** (e.g., stops if stunned, flees if alarmed).
\- You can have **1–5 echoes** depending on upgrades.

\### 2) Noise as a Resource
\- Every surface has a noise value (tile, metal, carpet).
\- Tools generate noise spikes (glass cutter, EMP, door wedge).
\- Higher difficulty: guards investigate patterns, not just single sounds.

\### 3) “Paradox Meter” (Risk/Reward)
\- Each echo increases paradox.  
\- High paradox gives score multipliers and rarer loot spawns, but triggers: extra cameras, faster guards, locked exits, “time warden” enemy.

\---

\## Progression (Roguelite)
\- **Meta upgrades:** longer echo duration, more echoes, quieter movement, faster hacking.
\- **Run upgrades (temporary):** gadgets, consumables, special masks with perks.
\- **Unlocks:** new facility types (Museum, Data Vault, Casino, Research Lab), new guard archetypes.

\---

\## Modes
\- **Solo:** you build your own echo crew.
\- **Co-op (2–4):** each player can record echoes; coordination becomes the skill ceiling.
\- **Daily Contract:** same seed for everyone; leaderboards based on stealth score and loot.

\---

\## Why It’s “Profitable” (from a content + retention angle)
\- Creates endless “I pulled off this insane synchronized heist” clips (viral-friendly).
\- Daily contracts + roguelite progression drive repeat sessions.
\- DLC-friendly: new biomes, gadgets, enemy types, modifiers.
\- Streamer-friendly: audience can vote on contract modifiers (“No EMP”, “Double guards”).

\---

\## Minimal Prototype Scope (4–6 weeks)
\- One facility tileset + procedural rooms
\- 3 guard types (patrol, camera operator, heavy)
\- 4 gadgets (noise maker, EMP, lockpick, smoke)
\- Echo system with 1–2 echoes max
\- Basic scoring + extraction

\## Math-Educational Game Design: **Echo Heist: Quant Vault**
A stealth/strategy roguelite where *every* action is powered by math skills. You don’t “pause to solve worksheets”—math is the control layer for planning, movement, hacking, loot optimization, and escape.

\### Target skills (wired into the structure)
\- Mental math (add/subtract/multiply/divide), estimation
\- Fractions/decimals/percents (risk, discounts, probabilities)
\- Ratios &amp; rates (speed, time, distance, resource burn)
\- Algebra basics (unknowns, balancing, formulas)
\- Geometry &amp; spatial reasoning (angles, area, shortest path)
\- Data literacy (charts, patterns, expected value)

\---

\## Core Loop (Math-first, not math-mini-game)
\### 1) Scout (Geometry + estimation)
\- Guard vision cones display as **angles + distance**.
\- You must choose routes using:
  \- **Shortest path** vs **lowest risk** (risk is numeric).
  \- “Noise budget” shown as a number (e.g., 100 max). Surfaces add noise costs.
\- Players learn to estimate: “This path is 34 noise vs 51 noise but saves 6 seconds.”

\### 2) Plan (Ratios, time windows, scheduling)
You plan synchronized “echo” actions by setting timers expressed as math constraints:
\- Door opens for **8 seconds** after switch.
\- Hack takes **t seconds**, where **t = base − (tool% × base)**.
\- You schedule echoes by matching inequalities:
  \- Example: “Echo A must reach panel in **≤ 12s** while Echo B distracts for **≥ 10s**.”

\### 3) Execute (Mental math under pressure)
Actions require quick, contextual math:
\- **Lockpick:** pick sequence is a running total; overshoot triggers alarm. (practice addition/subtraction + negative numbers later)
\- **Laser grid:** rotate mirrors by degrees; must satisfy angle rules. (geometry)
\- **Camera loop:** choose the correct *rate* to desync footage (e.g., 0.75× for 12s). (ratios/decimals)

\### 4) Loot &amp; Escape (Percents, optimization, expected value)
Loot isn’t “grab all”—you solve *optimization*:
\- Bag capacity = 12 units.
\- Items have **value**, **weight**, **heat** (risk).
\- Selling has **fees** and **percent-based multipliers** for clean runs.
\- You decide based on **value per weight** and **expected value** under risk.

\---

\## Systems That Reinforce Math Constantly

\### A) “Math as Keys” (the core gating mechanic)
Every locked system has a **math key**. Not trivia—real gameplay decisions.

\**Examples**
1\. **Prime Keypads (number sense)**
   \- Door accepts only numbers with specific properties:
   \- “Enter a number between 40–70 that is divisible by 3 but not by 2.”
2\. **Equation Locks (algebra)**
   \- Balance a simple equation to generate the code:
   \- 3x + 5 = 20 → x = 5 (code = 05)
3\. **Fraction Valves (fractions/LCM)**
   \- Set three valves to sum to 1 exactly using fractions.

Difficulty adapts: early game uses small numbers; later introduces decimals, multi-step constraints, or time pressure.

\---

\### B) “Skill Trees” are Math Skill Trees
Upgrades are framed as tools, but they map to skill mastery.

\**Examples**
\- **Estimator Mask:** highlights “close enough” answers accepted (teaches rounding/estimation).
\- **Algebra Rig:** lets you store one variable and reuse it (encourages forming expressions).
\- **Percent Gloves:** instantly converts discount/tax/bonus without calculator after repeated mastery (game gradually reduces hints).

\---

\### C) Adaptive Mastery (personalized reinforcement)
Each player has a hidden mastery profile:
\- If you struggle with fractions, the game increases fraction-based interactions *but lowers pressure* and gives more scaffolding.
\- If you master percents, it introduces more optimization problems (expected value, compounding bonuses).

\**Scaffolding options**
\- Visual fraction bars
\- Number lines
\- Step-by-step hints that cost score (so strong players skip)

\---

\## Example Mission (shows math everywhere)
\**Objective:** Steal the “Sigma Gem” from a museum vault.

\- **Entry door:** “Code is the GCD of 84 and 126.” (GCD = 42)
\- **Security grid:** rotate reflectors to match **incident angle = reflection angle**.
\- **Display case:** requires setting mixture:
  \- “Neutralize acid by creating a 12% solution from 20% concentrate + water.” (percents / ratios)
\- **Escape van:** fuel drain rate problem:
  \- “Van travels 3.5 km/min for 4 min—do you reach the bridge before lockdown at 13 min remaining?” (rates/time)
