# Level Role Map

## Purpose
Force every level to have one job. Catch progression walls and overloaded levels.

## Use this when
Before or during P2B. Also when a playtest reveals a "wall" or "unfair spike."

## Controls
- Every level must have one primary job
- New cognitive demands require lowering speed, clutter, penalty, or simultaneous choices
- If a level feels like a wall, define its role before tuning it
- No level should add more than one new demand

## Do not use this for
- Visual or personality decisions (those are P3)
- Replay or variation decisions (those are P4)
- Deciding whether the game concept works (that is P0)

## Bakery Rush

| Level | Teaches | Tests | New demand | Must NOT introduce yet | Why next level exists |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | The loop: tap pastries, match target | Can you add +1 items to a small number? | Belt movement, patience bar | Mixed values, speed pressure | L1 proves the mechanic works before complexity |
| L2 | Larger targets need more items | Can you track a running total to 10? | Higher targets (6-10) | Mixed pastry values | L2 proves counting works before value-weighting |
| L3 | Long counts require working memory | Can you maintain a sum past 10 under time? | Targets 11-15, faster belt | Mixed values, belt gaps | L3 proves counting under pressure before adding decision complexity |
| L4 | Different pastries have different values | Can you choose +1 vs +2 strategically? | Cookie (+1) and Cake Slice (+2) | Drink (+5), belt jolt | L4 proves value-weighted selection before high-value items |
| L5 | Three value types require real arithmetic | Can you combine +1, +2, +5 under full speed? | Drink (+5), full speed, all pressure | Nothing — this is the mastery test | L5 proves the full game |

## Fire Dispatch

| Level | Teaches | Tests | New demand | Must NOT introduce yet | Why next level exists |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | Subset-sum with 3 trucks | Can you find a combination of 3 that matches demand? | Timer (20s), demands 4-8 | Wrong-dispatch penalty | L1 proves the mechanic with a small set |

## How to use this document

Before P2B, for every level ask:
1. What is this level's one job?
2. Does it introduce only one new demand?
3. Can I explain why the next level exists?

If a level introduces two new demands, split them. If a level has no clear job, it is probably filler.

### Red flags
- A level adds new representation AND faster speed → split into two levels
- A level's job is "harder" with no specific new skill → it is probably a speed increase disguised as progression
- A level removes a tool the player relied on (e.g., removing Ambulance at L4) → confusing, not challenging
