# Family Map — Math Concepts

This file maps the strongest current math concepts into likely game families.

The goal is not to lock every concept permanently.
The goal is to make the product structure clearer before more prototypes are built.

---

## Why this matters

The strongest monetizable path is likely not a set of unrelated games.

It is likely a set of **families**:
- shared logic
- shared interaction patterns
- shared audience logic
- room for multiple related products

This helps with:
- product sequencing
- engine reuse
- clearer platform messaging
- expansion without chaos

---

## Family 1 — Quantity and Fulfillment

### Core idea
The player fulfills an exact quantity target by placing or adding items into a container or destination.

### Shared interaction pattern
Usually **Combine and Build**

### Core player feeling
Accuracy, completion, practical quantity matching

### Strongest current concepts
- Bakery Rush
- Pharmacy Counter

### Why this family is strong
This is the cleanest current family because:
- the math is directly in the action
- the loop is easy to understand
- it works well for early elementary
- it has both parent and classroom potential
- it can expand without changing the core logic

### Strategic note
This is the best family to prove first.

### Open decision
Pharmacy Counter likely belongs here, but must still be tested to decide whether it is:
- a sibling concept
- a level-up path
- or a more advanced branch

Current best hypothesis:
**sibling concept in the same family**

## Family 2 — Proportional Reasoning

### Core idea
The player adjusts quantities or proportions to match a target outcome.

### Shared interaction pattern
Usually **Allocate and Balance**

### Core player feeling
Adjustment, tuning, proportional correctness

### Strongest current concepts
- Chef’s Fractions

### Why this family matters
This family helps the platform grow beyond early counting and addition.

It introduces:
- fractions
- scaling
- measurement logic
- real-world mathematical usefulness

### Strategic note
This is likely the strongest second family after Quantity and Fulfillment.

---

## Family 3 — Coordinate Navigation

### Core idea
The player moves, selects, or plots positions correctly in space.

### Shared interaction pattern
Usually **Navigate and Position**

### Core player feeling
Navigation, spatial accuracy, directional correctness

### Strongest current concepts
- Galaxy Coordinates
- Archaeology Grid

### Why this family matters
This family is a strong middle-grade expansion because:
- it feels distinctly different from arithmetic
- it stays visually teachable
- it has clear STEM value
- it can scale into richer spatial reasoning later

### Strategic note
Galaxy Coordinates is the strongest current candidate in this family.

---

## Family 4 — Financial Decision Math

### Core idea
The player interprets numerical change and makes a decision based on relative performance.

### Shared interaction pattern
Likely **Allocate and Balance** or a refined future family such as rank-and-decide

### Core player feeling
Comparison, urgency, judgment under pressure

### Strongest current concepts
- Trade Floor

### Why this family matters
This family may become commercially powerful because it feels modern and differentiated.

### Strategic note
This concept is attractive, but still less structurally clean than Bakery or Galaxy.
It likely needs more loop sharpening before it becomes a prototype priority.

---

## Family 5 — Compare and Order

### Core idea
The player computes small values, compares their magnitudes, and places items in a ranked order (ascending or descending). The core cognitive act is compute → compare → rank-order, which is structurally distinct from exact-sum composition and from pattern prediction.

### Shared interaction pattern
**Sequence and Order** (canonical interaction type `sequence_and_order`).

### Core player feeling
Comparison, ranking, ordering confidence.

### Strongest current concepts
- Snack Line Shuffle (first K–2 proof case)

### Why this family matters
This family fills the widest current portfolio gap at once:
- a K–2 grade band (no other current concept lives here)
- a distinct cognitive act that does not reduce to either Quantity and Fulfillment or Sequence and Predict
- room for future K–12 ordering games across multiple math domains without forcing them into prediction or composition framings

### Strategic note
Snack Line Shuffle is the first K–2 member of this family and the first proof case for the canonical `sequence_and_order` interaction type. Family coverage must **not** be marked as fully proven until a P1 prototype exists and passes its Definition of Done — this entry establishes the family structurally, not evidentially.

## Strategic conclusion

Do not build isolated games one by one with no family logic.

Use Bakery to prove the first family.

Then use that family structure to decide:
- what expands naturally
- what shares engine logic
- what becomes a separate family
- what should wait

For now, the clearest sequence is:
1. prove Quantity and Fulfillment
2. expand into Proportional Reasoning
3. then expand into Coordinate Navigation

---

## What success looks like

A good family map should help answer:

1. Can the first family support more than one game?
2. Can the OS support more than one major interaction type?
3. Which concept is easiest to monetize first?
4. Which concepts deserve full product development later?
5. Which ideas are exciting but should wait?
