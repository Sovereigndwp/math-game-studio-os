# Prototype Sequence — Math Concepts

This file defines the current recommended prototype sequence for the strongest math concepts.

The goal is to avoid scattered builds and keep the product path focused.

---

## Guiding rule

Do not choose the next prototype based only on theme excitement.

Choose the next prototype based on:
- structural clarity
- family value
- monetization potential
- what the current system is best prepared to support

---

## Recommended sequence

### 1. Bakery Rush
**Why first**
- strongest current concept
- already the most developed
- clearest loop
- easiest first prototype
- strongest family seed
- best balance of educational strength and commercial clarity

**What this prototype should prove**
- the OS can produce a real playable first game
- the combine-and-build arithmetic loop works
- the quantity-and-fulfillment family is worth building further

**Why it matters**
Bakery is not just one game.
It is the proof case for the whole system.

---

### 2. Pharmacy Counter
**Why second**
- likely the strongest sibling concept to Bakery
- tests whether the first family is real
- increases precision and seriousness without leaving the same logic family
- creates room for stronger later variations

**Main strategic question**
Should this live:
- inside Bakery as progression
- beside Bakery as a sibling game
- or as a separate branch?

**Current best hypothesis**
Sibling concept in the same family

**Why it matters**
This is the first real test of family expansion.

---

### 3. Galaxy Coordinates
**Why third**
- strongest middle-grade concept
- different enough from Bakery to prove the OS is not trapped in early arithmetic
- clear family logic
- strong STEM appeal
- visually understandable

**What this prototype should prove**
- the OS can support a second major interaction family
- coordinate navigation can become a viable middle-grade path

---

### 4. Chef’s Fractions
**Why fourth**
- very strong educational concept
- clear real-world usefulness
- strong school and parent appeal
- likely important long-term family

**Why not before Galaxy**
It is strong, but Bakery → Pharmacy → Galaxy creates a cleaner progression:
- prove family
- prove second family type
- then deepen a new family around proportions

Still, this could move earlier if future evaluation says the fraction loop is cleaner than expected.

---

### 5. Trade Floor
**Why fifth**
- strongest commercial hook after Bakery
- differentiated concept
- strong later-stage appeal
- potential bridge into financial literacy products

**Why later**
- loop is still less clean
- risk of becoming more dashboard than game
- should follow after stronger family proof

---

## What should not be built yet

Do not build these before the five above are clarified:

- Bridge Load
- Archaeology Grid
- broader cross-subject ideas
- any non-math concept family

This is not because they are bad.
It is because the current path should stay focused.

---

## Prototype sequence logic in one line

### First prove the family
Bakery → Pharmacy

### Then prove a second family
Galaxy

### Then deepen the product system
Chef’s Fractions

### Then test a higher-risk commercial concept
Trade Floor

---

## What success looks like

A good prototype sequence should help answer:

1. Can the first family support more than one game?
2. Can the OS support more than one major interaction type?
3. Which concept is easiest to monetize first?
4. Which concepts deserve full product development later?
5. Which ideas are exciting but should wait?

## Strategic conclusion

Do not try to prove the entire Brain Games platform at once.

Use the math concepts to prove:
- one strong family
- one second family
- one repeatable product path

If that works, the broader platform vision becomes much more believable.
