Use this packet as normalized input for concept evaluation inside Math Game Studio OS.

For now, this packet should stay focused on math-only concepts.

Do not broaden into language, science, or cross-disciplinary concepts during this evaluation pass.

For each concept below, Claude should:

1. treat the concept brief as the input idea

# 1. Bakery Rush

## Concept Name
Bakery Rush

## Subject
Math

## Age Band
Grades 1 to 3

## Career Anchor
Baker / Bakery Assistant

## Core Skill
Addition within 20

## Secondary Skills
Counting on, number bonds, quantity tracking

## One Sentence Concept
Players fill bakery orders by adding pastries into a box until the total matches the customer's order exactly.

## Why This Feels Real
A bakery gives counting and order fulfillment a clear real-world purpose.

## Likely Strong Interaction Type
Combine and Build

## Core Loop Hypothesis
A customer order shows a target number. The player taps pastries into a box. The running total updates. If the total matches exactly, the order is complete. If the player goes over, the last pastry bounces out.

## Family Hypothesis
Quantity and Fulfillment Family

## Prototype Scope Hint
Single screen, one order at a time, visible target, tap pastries, running total, exact-match success, overshoot bounce-back, simple replay into the next order.

## Monetization Notes
Very strong first product candidate for parents, early elementary classrooms, and later family expansion.

## Main Question Claude Should Answer
Is this still the strongest first prototype candidate after full OS evaluation?

# 2. Pharmacy Counter

## Concept Name
Pharmacy Counter

## Subject
Math

## Age Band
Grades 1 to 3

## Career Anchor
Pharmacist / Pharmacy Technician

## Core Skill
Counting and addition within 20

## Secondary Skills
Precision, quantity matching, first-try accuracy

## One Sentence Concept
Players fill prescription bottles by counting the exact number of pill tiles required for each order.

## Why This Feels Real
Prescription filling makes exact quantity and accuracy feel important.

## Likely Strong Interaction Type
Combine and Build

## Core Loop Hypothesis
A prescription shows a target quantity. The player taps pill tiles into a bottle. The count updates. When the quantity matches exactly, the player dispenses the prescription.

## Family Hypothesis
Quantity and Fulfillment Family, likely related to Bakery Rush

## Prototype Scope Hint
Single prescription at a time, visible target quantity, tap pills into bottle, running count, dispense only on exact count.

## Monetization Notes
Strong family expansion candidate. Could become either a sibling game to Bakery or a stronger later branch with more precision and grouped quantities.

## Main Question Claude Should Answer
Should this become a level-up path inside Bakery, a sibling concept in the same family, or a separate family?

# 3. Chef's Fractions

## Concept Name
Chef's Fractions

## Subject
Math

## Age Band
Grades 3 to 5

## Career Anchor
Chef / Culinary Arts

## Core Skill
Fractions and scaling recipes

## Secondary Skills
Measurement, ratio sense, proportional reasoning

## One Sentence Concept
Players scale ingredients up or down so a recipe serves the correct number of guests.

## Why This Feels Real
Cooking gives fractions a practical purpose that children can picture.

## Likely Strong Interaction Type
Allocate and Balance

## Core Loop Hypothesis
The player sees the original recipe and the new serving size. They adjust ingredient amounts until all measurements correctly match the new order.

## Family Hypothesis
Proportional Reasoning Family

## Prototype Scope Hint
Single recipe at a time, small number of ingredients, simple plus/minus or slider adjustment, submit when all adjusted values are correct.

## Monetization Notes
Strong school and parent appeal because fractions are hard for many students and this theme is easy to explain.

## Main Question Claude Should Answer
Does this concept cleanly become the second family after Bakery, or does it still need loop sharpening first?

# 4. Galaxy Coordinates

## Concept Name
Galaxy Coordinates

## Subject
Math

## Age Band
Grades 5 to 8

## Career Anchor
Astrophysicist / Mission Planner

## Core Skill
Coordinate geometry

## Secondary Skills
Graphing, ordered pairs, spatial reasoning

## One Sentence Concept
Players redirect probes by plotting the correct coordinates on a space grid.

## Why This Feels Real
Mission planning makes graphing feel like navigation with consequences.

## Likely Strong Interaction Type
Navigate and Position

## Core Loop Hypothesis
The player reads a mission instruction, identifies the correct coordinate, and selects the right location on the grid to keep the probe on course.

## Family Hypothesis

## Prototype Scope Hint

## Main Question Claude Should Answer
Should this be the first middle-grade concept that follows Bakery?

# 5. Trade Floor

## Concept Name
Trade Floor

## Subject
Math

## Age Band
Grades 5 to 8

## Career Anchor
Financial Analyst / Economist

## Core Skill
Ratios, percents, and comparative data reading

## Secondary Skills
Decision making, ranking, interpreting change

## One Sentence Concept
Players compare asset performance, rank changes, and make quick buy, hold, or sell decisions.

## Why This Feels Real
Markets make percent change and relative performance feel urgent and useful.

## Likely Strong Interaction Type
Allocate and Balance or Rank and Compare

## Core Loop Hypothesis
The player sees several assets with performance data, ranks them by change, and chooses the strongest action based on visible evidence.

## Family Hypothesis
Financial Decision Family

## Prototype Scope Hint
Start with ranking by percent change before adding buy, hold, or sell decisions. Keep the first prototype narrow.

## Monetization Notes
Very strong commercial angle, but the loop may still be less pure than Bakery or Galaxy and may need refinement.

## Main Question Claude Should Answer
Is this one of the most commercially attractive concepts even if it is not yet one of the cleanest prototype candidates?
