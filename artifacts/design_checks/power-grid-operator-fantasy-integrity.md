# Fantasy Integrity: Power Grid Operator

**Fantasy strength:** 0.85

**A. Role necessity — strong**
The grid operator role naturally requires computing missing power values and deciding where to route them. You cannot keep the city alive without solving the algebra. The role justifies the math without forcing it.

**B. World logic — strong**
Districts have demand. Sources have capacity. The gap between supply and demand is the missing variable. The world's physics (electricity must balance) makes the math feel inevitable. "5 + x = 18" isn't an abstract equation — it's "how much battery power prevents a blackout."

**C. Decision meaning — strong**
The theme changes what choices mean. Routing 13 MW to Downtown means Harbor District doesn't get it. The same equation (solve for x = 13) has different strategic meaning depending on which districts are degrading and which sources are available. If the theme were swapped, this allocation layer would lose its meaning.

**D. Emotional fit — strong**
Districts going dark creates real stakes — sirens, citizen reactions, the map dimming. A grid operator preventing blackouts has inherent drama that "solve for x" alone cannot create. The player feels responsible for the city, not just for getting the right number.

**E. Replaceability test — integral**
If the theme were swapped to (e.g.) "solve equations to fill containers," the allocation decision between competing districts would lose all meaning. The grid topology, the source types (solar, wind, battery), the district identities — all of these make the allocation decisions non-trivial. The math is embedded in the world, not decorated by it.

**Strongest fantasy element:**
The allocation decision — choosing which district gets power when you can't power all of them — is the layer above correctness that makes this more than a quiz.

**Strongest fantasy weakness:**
If generators are interchangeable (any source powers any district equally), the allocation becomes trivial. The game needs source-type constraints (e.g., battery is limited, solar depends on weather) to keep allocation meaningful.

**Main worksheet-in-costume risk:**
If every level has enough power for every district (no scarcity), the game reduces to "solve equations in order" with no strategic layer. Scarcity must be real.

**Recommendation before build:**
Ensure that by L3+, the player faces at least one moment per shift where they must choose which district to power and which to let brownout. If the allocation is always obvious, the fantasy is decoration.
